Question：Looking back at the whole semester, what comes to mind regarding your experience with Class PRO and Class Ready in this SE master program?
Answer：For me, these tools helped a lot compared to last semester, when we relied only on textbooks and teacher-provided materials. Class PRO significantly improved my speaking skills, character recognition, and listening ability. Before class, I had to go through online preparation—answering questions first, then reviewing in class—which trained me to listen carefully and think actively. This process built mental discipline and made me much more serious and focused during learning.
Question：How has your confidence in participating in class changed after using Class Ready?
Answer：I feel far more confident now. Earlier, I often hesitated before speaking up—I wasn’t sure whether I’d participate or not. But after completing Class Ready tasks, I enter class fully prepared and 100% certain I’ll take part. That shift came from knowing the vocabulary, structures, and content in advance.
Question：Can you describe a specific moment when you realized Class Ready content connected meaningfully to real-life Chinese usage?
Answer：Yes—I remember one phrase taught in Class Ready that was actually used in daily life: a saying involving mountains, something like “we can climb a mountain.” At first, it felt abstract, but later I realized it appeared naturally in conversations and even in media. That moment made me take Chinese more seriously, because it showed me the material wasn’t just academic—it worked in reality. I started spending more time on Class Ready, usually 30 to 45 minutes per session.
Question：How do you typically use Class Ready before class, and what steps do you follow?
Answer：I start by reviewing vocabulary three or four times, then test myself without any support—I click to reveal characters and try to recall their meanings and pronunciations. If I remember them well, I move to the sentence-building section. There, I read each question carefully to understand its meaning—for example, if it mentions cities like Beijing, Shanghai, or Xi’an, I figure out what kind of response it expects. Once I grasp the intent, I construct sentences from memory, without referring to notes or translations.
Question：How has Class Ready affected your ability to understand and respond to spoken Chinese outside class?
Answer：It’s improved my real-time listening and speaking reactions. Now, when I meet Chinese speakers outside class, I can recognize what they’re saying and attempt to reply—even if they speak quickly, I can ask them to slow down. Before, listening was extremely difficult; last semester, I spent excessive time replaying audio clips with no transcript. With Class Ready, I use headphones, listen carefully, try to understand the meaning first, and only translate if I’m unsure. Then I check my comprehension against the correct answers—whether filling blanks or selecting options—and reflect on where I succeeded or missed. The third section, especially, strengthened my accuracy and confidence in interpreting spoken language.
